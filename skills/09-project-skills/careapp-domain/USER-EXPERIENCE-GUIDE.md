# Caregiving Companion - Complete User Experience Guide

## 🎯 Overview: What Can I Do With This App?

As a caregiver, this app becomes your 24/7 assistant that understands your loved one's needs. You can talk to it like a knowledgeable friend who never forgets anything and is always ready to help.

---

## 🚀 Getting Started: Your First Day

### Step 1: Sign Up & Choose Your Plan

1. **Visit the website** → Click "Get Started Free"
2. **Create your account** with email or Google sign-in
3. **Start 14-day free trial** (no credit card required initially)
4. **Choose your plan** based on voice needs:
   - **Starter ($19)**: 30 voice minutes - good for check-ins
   - **Essential ($49)**: 150 voice minutes - daily active care
   - **Professional ($99)**: 400 voice minutes - intensive care needs

### Step 2: Initial Setup (5 minutes)

1. **Tell Sarah about your loved one**:
   - "Sarah, I'm caring for my mom, Helen, she's 78"
   - "She has diabetes and mild dementia"
   - "She lives alone about 20 minutes away"

2. **Sarah responds**:
   - "I understand. I'll help you manage Helen's care. Let's start by setting up her medication schedule. What medications does she take?"

3. **Add medications** (just by talking):
   - "She takes Metformin twice daily, 500mg with breakfast and dinner"
   - "Lisinopril once in the morning, 10mg"
   - Sarah confirms and sets up automatic reminders

## 💬 Primary Interface: Talking to Sarah

### The Magic: Natural Conversation

**You don't need to learn commands. Just talk naturally:**

#### Example Morning Routine:

```
You: "Good morning Sarah, how's mom doing?"

Sarah: "Good morning! Helen took her morning Metformin at 8:15 AM -
I called to remind her. She has a cardiology appointment tomorrow
at 2 PM. Would you like me to call her this afternoon to remind
her to take her evening medication?"

You: "Yes please, and can you check if she needs anything from
the pharmacy?"

Sarah: "I'll call her at 5 PM for the medication reminder and
ask about pharmacy needs. I'll text you right after the call
with what she needs."
```

### Voice Access Options:

1. **In-App Voice** (bottom of screen): Click microphone or say "Hey Sarah"
2. **Phone Calls**: Sarah can call you or your loved one
3. **Text Chat**: Type if you can't talk
4. **SMS**: Text Sarah when away from app

## 📋 Feature-by-Feature User Guide

## 1. 💊 **Medication Management**

### How to Use:

1. **Adding Medications** (voice or type):
   - "Add Metformin, 500mg, twice daily with meals"
   - Sarah asks clarifying questions if needed
   - Automatically creates reminder schedule

2. **Daily Medication Flow**:
   - **8:00 AM**: Sarah calls Mom: "Good morning Helen! Time for your morning medications"
   - **8:15 AM**: Mom confirms taken → Sarah logs it
   - **8:16 AM**: You get notification: "✓ Helen took morning meds"
   - **If missed**: Sarah calls you after 30 minutes

3. **Checking Status**:
   - "Did mom take her medications today?"
   - "Show me this week's medication compliance"
   - "What medications is she running low on?"

4. **Refill Management**:
   - Sarah tracks pill counts
   - Alerts you 7 days before running out
   - Can call pharmacy for refills (with permission)

### Visual Dashboard Shows:

- Today's medication schedule with checkmarks
- Weekly compliance chart
- Upcoming refills needed
- Medication interaction warnings

---

## 2. 📅 **Appointment Management**

### How to Use:

1. **Scheduling** (just tell Sarah):
   - "Mom has a cardiology appointment next Tuesday at 2 PM"
   - "She needs to see the dentist sometime next month"
   - Sarah adds to calendar and plans reminders

2. **Automatic Reminder Sequence**:
   - **1 week before**: Email reminder to you
   - **2 days before**: Sarah calls Mom to confirm
   - **Day before**: Text reminder to both
   - **Day of (morning)**: Call to Mom with prep instructions
   - **2 hours before**: Final reminder call

3. **Transportation Coordination**:
   - "I can't drive mom to her appointment"
   - Sarah suggests: "Shall I book an Uber for Helen?"
   - Coordinates pickup/dropoff times
   - Texts you when Mom arrives

4. **Post-Appointment Follow-up**:
   - Sarah calls Mom: "How did your appointment go?"
   - Records any new medications or instructions
   - Updates care plan accordingly

---

## 3. 🏠 **Daily Check-ins & Wellness Calls**

### How It Works:

1. **Morning Wellness Call** (customizable time):
   - Sarah calls Mom: "Good morning Helen! How are you feeling today?"
   - Asks about pain levels, sleep, appetite
   - Reminds about daily tasks
   - Assesses cognitive state through conversation

2. **You Get Summary**:
   - "Helen is doing well today. Good spirits, slept 7 hours, appetite normal. She mentioned some knee pain (4/10). She's looking forward to lunch with her friend Mary."

3. **Emergency Detection**:
   - If Mom doesn't answer after 3 attempts
   - If she sounds confused or distressed
   - Sarah immediately alerts you and can call 911

### Customizable Check-ins:

- **Frequency**: Daily, twice daily, or weekly
- **Topics**: Medications, meals, mood, activities
- **Duration**: 2-10 minutes per call
- **Language**: English, Spanish, others

## 5. 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 **Family Coordination**

### How to Share Care:

1. **Invite Family Members**:

2. **Role-Based Access**:
   - **Primary Caregiver**: Full access, all features

## 🔄 **How Features Work Together**

### Scenario: Managing a Health Crisis

**Day 1 - Mom Falls**

- You: "Sarah, Mom fell and is at the ER"
- Sarah: "I'm documenting this. Do you need her medication list?"
- Automatically: Notifies family, prepares medical history

**Day 2 - Hospital Stay**

- Sarah tracks medications given at hospital
- Coordinates family visits via shared calendar
- Documents doctor instructions

**Day 3 - Discharge Planning**

- Sarah helps schedule follow-up appointments
- Updates medication list with changes
- Arranges transportation home

**Day 4+ - Recovery**

- Increased check-in calls
- Medication compliance monitoring
- PT appointment reminders
- Progress reports to family

### Everything Connected:

- **Health feeds into Tasks**: New medication → automatic reminders
- **Tasks trigger Calls**: Reminder due → Sarah calls
- **Calls update Health**: Mom reports pain → logged in health record
- **All creates Documentation**: Automatic care journal maintained
