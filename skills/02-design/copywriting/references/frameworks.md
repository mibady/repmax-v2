# Copywriting Frameworks Reference

## Foundation: The Three Brains

**Reptilian (Survival):** Processes tone/energy before words. Asks: "Is this safe?"
**Limbic (Emotion):** The actual decision-maker. Asks: "How does this feel?"
**Neocortex (Logic):** Justifies emotional decisions. Asks: "How do I explain this?"

**Principle:** Emotion decides, logic justifies. Win the limbic system first.

## Emotion Map

**Approach Emotions (Move Toward):**
- Joy/Happiness → "Imagine how good it will feel when..."
- Excitement → New releases, exclusive access, "coming soon"
- Hope → Transformation stories, "Imagine if..."
- Trust → Guarantees, testimonials, credentials
- Pride → "You deserve...", "You've earned..."
- Curiosity → Mystery, teasers, incomplete information
- Relief → Solution after pain amplification

**Avoidance Emotions (Create Urgency):**
- Fear → "What happens if you DON'T act?"
- Frustration → "Aren't you tired of..."
- Regret → "What if you'd started last year?"
- Envy → "Your competitors are already using this"

**Warning:** Confused minds say NO. Simplify immediately.

## Structural Frameworks

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
**Problem:** Identify the pain point
**Agitate:** Amplify emotional impact, consequences
**Solution:** Present your offer as the bridge

*Best for: Cold emails, ads, short landing pages*

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
**Attention:** Pattern interrupt, hook
**Interest:** Expand with benefits, curiosity
**Desire:** Future pace, social proof, emotional triggers
**Action:** Clear CTA with urgency

*Best for: Emails, ads, landing pages, presentations*

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
**Before:** Current painful state
**After:** Desired transformed state
**Bridge:** Your solution connects them

*Best for: Transformation messaging, weight loss, business growth*

### FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)
**Features:** What it has/does
**Advantages:** Why that matters technically
**Benefits:** Emotional outcome (what they FEEL)

*Best for: Technical products, B2B SaaS, feature-rich offers*

### The 4 P's (Problem-Promise-Proof-Proposal)
**Problem:** Acknowledge their pain
**Promise:** Specific outcome + timeframe
**Proof:** Social proof, case studies, data
**Proposal:** Clear offer + next step

*Best for: Sales calls, webinars, presentations*

### Star-Story-Solution
**Star:** Relatable person (ideally like the prospect)
**Story:** Their journey through the problem
**Solution:** How your offer created transformation

*Best for: Case studies, testimonials, VSLs*

## The Objection Hierarchy

**Surface objections decode to deeper concerns:**

| They Say | They Mean |
|----------|-----------|
| "Too expensive" | "I'm not convinced of value" OR "I'm scared" |
| "Need to think about it" | "I need more info" OR "I don't trust you yet" |
| "Talk to partner" | "I don't have authority" OR "I want backup" |
| "Not a good time" | "Not a priority" OR "I don't trust you" |
| "Send info" | "Go away" OR "I need to justify to others" |

**The 5 Deep Objections:**

1. **NO MONEY** → Payment plans, smaller packages
2. **NO AUTHORITY** → "Who else needs to be involved?"
3. **NO NEED** → Go back, find the pain
4. **NO TRUST** → More proof, guarantees, risk reversal
5. **NO URGENCY** → Scarcity, consequences of delay

**Response Protocol:** Acknowledge → Permission → Isolate → Uncover → Address

**Feel-Felt-Found:** "I understand how you FEEL. Many clients FELT the same. Here's what they FOUND..."

## The Value Equation

```
VALUE = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort Required)
```

**To increase value:**
- ↑ Dream Outcome - Make transformation bigger, more specific
- ↑ Perceived Likelihood - More proof, guarantees, authority
- ↓ Time Delay - "Results in 30 days" beats "6 months"
- ↓ Effort Required - "Done for you" beats "DIY"

**Principle:** Price only matters when value is unclear.

## 7 Stages of Buyer Awareness

| Stage | Status | Your Job |
|-------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Unaware | Educate, create problem awareness |
| 2 | Problem Aware | Make them solution aware |
| 3 | Solution Aware | Differentiate your solution |
| 4 | Product Aware | Prove value, handle objections |
| 5 | Most Aware | Create urgency, make buying easy |

**Critical:** Match message to awareness level or fail.

## Loss Aversion Architecture

Frame as avoiding loss, not gaining benefit:

**Instead of:** "Save $500/month"
**Use:** "Stop losing $500/month"

**Instead of:** "Get more customers"
**Use:** "Stop leaving money on the table"

**Pattern:**
1. Quantify current loss (time, money, opportunity)
2. Project loss over time (daily → monthly → yearly)
3. Compare to investment (loss > cost = easy decision)
4. Create urgency (every day = more loss)
